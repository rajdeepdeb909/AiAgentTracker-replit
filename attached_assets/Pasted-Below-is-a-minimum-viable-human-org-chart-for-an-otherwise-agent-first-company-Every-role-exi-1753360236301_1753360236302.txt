Below is a “minimum‑viable **human org‑chart**” for an otherwise agent‑first company.
Every role exists because it solves *one of four things AI can’t yet do well*:

1. **Hands‑on physical work** (repairing a fridge).
2. **Deep craft judgment** under novel edge‑cases (diagnosing a sealed‑system leak no LLM has seen).
3. **High‑stakes empathy or trust** (calming an irate homeowner who just lost all the food in their freezer).
4. **Policy, safety, or brand stewardship** (deciding whether an LLM‑devised price move is fair‑use or price‑gouging).

### Why humans stay critical *and* happy

* **Leverage, not load** – Each person touches *only* the 5 % of cases where judgment, dexterity, or empathy create disproportionate value; AI covers the rote 95 %.
* **Clear KPIs** – Every role has a small set of metrics the agents compute automatically; people spend time improving results, not proving results.
* **Career paths** – Field techs can graduate to Master Tech, Coach, or Brand Prompt Designer—blending hands‑on skill with AI stewardship.
* **Two‑sided customer obsession** – Homeowners get near‑instant, data‑driven service; technicians (our supply‑side “customers”) get predictable earnings, low admin noise, continuous up‑skilling, and human safety nets when the bot isn’t enough.

This hybrid blueprint keeps the head‑count *tiny* relative to a traditional service network, yet inserts human expertise exactly where it multiplies profit, retention, and trust—for both ends of the marketplace.
